With more and more businesses offering remote positions, it’s only natural that many people are looking for work-from-home jobs.

After all, work-from-home jobs provide more flexibility and help you save time and money by eliminating long and tiring commutes.

Nonetheless, picking the best work-from-home job can be a bit tough, and even more so if you have any special conditions in mind (e.g. if you’re a student looking for a part-time job).

Well, worry not - we’ve compiled a list of the 53 best work-from-home jobs to help you with your job search!

In this article, we will cover:

  • 53 Best Work From Home Jobs
  • Highest-Paying Work From Home Jobs
  • No Experience Work From Home Jobs
  • Part-Time Work From Home Jobs
  • Work From Home Jobs for Seniors
  • 5 Tips to Successfully Work From Home

So, let’s dive right in!

53 Best Work From Home Jobs 

Without further ado, here are the 53 best work from home jobs:

Highest-Paying Work From Home Jobs 

Wondering which work-from-home jobs pay the most?

Here are the 17 highest-paying work from home jobs:

#1. Cloud Architecture

Annual pay: $87,000 - $181,000 

Cloud architects manage cloud computing systems, create and implement cloud strategies, and ensure cloud systems work smoothly.

By 2025, more than 8 out of 10 businesses will primarily rely on cloud computing, bringing plenty of job opportunities for all cloud architects out there.

#2. Algorithm Engineering

Annual pay: $105,000 - $158,000

If you have several programming languages and machine learning experience listed on your engineering resume, algorithm engineering might be the perfect remote job for you!

Simply put, algorithm engineers are responsible for developing, testing, and improving AI algorithms.

#3. Clinical Psychology

Annual pay: $53,000 - $153,000 

Clinical psychologists test, diagnose, and treat mental, emotional, social, and behavioral disorders.

Working remotely has many benefits for clinical psychologists (e.g. helping reach patients in rural and underserved areas), making the profession one of the best high-paying work-from-home jobs.

#4. Data Science

Annual pay: $68,000 - $146,000 

Data science is a rapidly growing field, which makes it one of the most in-demand and high-paying work-from-home jobs.

Data scientists gather, organize, and analyze data as well as manage databases.

#5. Business Development Management

Annual pay: $48,000 - $142,000

The main responsibilities of business development managers include setting business goals and developing strategies for business and revenue growth.

So, if you have strong communication skills alongside a degree in business, marketing, or another related field, a business development manager might be one of the best work-from-home jobs for you.

#6. Software Engineering

Annual pay: $64,000 - $139,000 

Software engineers write, test, debug, and maintain computer software. 

Now, here’s the deal - the average annual salary of entry-level software engineers is $77,000.

As such, if you’re looking for high-paying work-from-home jobs in the IT field, you really can’t go wrong with software engineering.

#7. Product Management

Annual pay: $59,000 - $136,000 

Do you have a passion for marketing and outstanding strategic planning skills? 

Well, product management may just be the right work-from-home job for you!

As a product manager, your main responsibilities include identifying the product vision, developing strategy, and making sure the product perfectly fits the market.

#8. Certified Public Accounting (CPA)

Annual pay: $50,000 - $125,000

If you’re an accountant aspiring to have a high-paying work-at-home job, you may feel your only option is to make a career change and switch to the IT sector. 

Well, here’s an easier solution - getting a CPA certification can increase your annual salary by 15%!

#9. Project Management

Annual pay: $50,000 - $121,000 

Are you a professional in managing time, budget, and teams?

Well, project management may be the ideal work-from-home job for you!

As a project manager, you’ll be responsible for planning, developing, and monitoring all aspects of a project remotely.

#10. Cyber Security Analytics

Annual pay: $54,000 - $119,000 

Cyber security analysts protect businesses against cyber attacks.

Because cyber security is a top priority for most companies, it’s not surprising that many businesses offer generous compensation to ensure their data stays safe and secure.

#11. User Experience (UX) Design

Annual pay: $55,000 - $117,000 

Simply put, UX designers are responsible for optimizing website usability and ensuring customer satisfaction.

So, if you can’t stand poorly designed websites, have an eye for detail, and have a background in IT, UX design might be right up your alley. Not to mention, it pays well!

#12. Software Development

Annual pay: $50,000 - $112,000 

Software developers use ready-made tools to develop, install, and test computer software and applications.

This high-paying position is perfect for you if you have a degree in computer science (or another related field), have a solid grasp of several programming languages, and are looking for a work-from-home job in a fast-paced environment.

#13. Marketing Management

Annual pay: $45,000 - $106,000 

If you’re a seasoned marketing professional, this work-from-home job is for you.

As a marketing manager, you’ll be responsible for developing and implementing traditional and digital marketing strategies to promote the business and drive more sales.

#14. Quality Assurance (QA) Engineering

Annual pay: $43,000 - $104,000

Essentially, QA engineers test software and ensure that it works perfectly before the launch.

So, if you have impressive analytical, problem-solving, and programming skills, you might want to give QA engineering a try.

#15. Speech-Language Pathology

Annual pay: $54,000 - $103,000 

Speech-language pathologists work with children and adults to prevent, diagnose, and treat speech, language, swallowing, and communication disorders.

Although most speech-language pathologists work in person, online speech therapy is becoming a thing as it facilitates the treatment by helping clients relax.

#16. Web Development

Annual pay: $41,000 - $92,000

Do you come from an IT background and value form as much as a function? If so, you might want to look into web development.

In short, web developers design, build, troubleshoot, and maintain websites. Unlike some other IT positions, web developers are responsible for the appearance and usability of the website.

#17. Recruitment

Annual pay: $37,000 - $82,000 

Recruiters determine hiring needs, create job descriptions, source candidates, screen resumes, conduct job interviews, and hire new employees.

As of today, nearly 90% of companies conduct job interviews online, so many recruiters switch from in-person to work-from-home positions.

No Experience Work From Home Jobs 

If you’re looking for a remote job that requires little to no experience, consider these 13 work from home jobs:

#1. Customer Support

Annual pay: $26,000 - $54,000

Customer support representatives respond to customer questions, handle complaints, and resolve any issues. 

As a work-from-home job, customer support is perfectly suited for anyone with an array of soft skills, including adaptability, composure, time management, and empathy.

#2. Virtual Travel Coordination

Annual pay: $31,000 - $60,000

Virtual travel coordinators help people plan their travel itineraries, book hotels, rental-cars and find the best travel deals. 

As such, being a virtual travel coordinator is the ideal work-from-home job for any avid traveler with impressive planning skills.

#3. Online Community Moderation

Annual pay: $32,000 - $110,000

Simply put, online community moderators monitor online discussions to ensure they meet community standards and comply with the rules. 

So, if you frequently participate in online forums and don’t mind dealing with problematic users, this may be the perfect work from home job for you!

#4. DogSitting

Annual pay:  $19,000 - $61,000

Dogsitting is one of the best work-from-home jobs for animal lovers.

Although this job doesn’t require any experience, you do need to have excellent pet care knowledge. 

Alternatively, you can also get training to provide pet grooming services.

#5. Makeup Artistry

Hourly rate: $12 - $51

Typically, makeup artists work in film, fashion, television, and theater.

However, if you’re skilled in applying makeup, you can become a freelance makeup artist and welcome clients in the comfort of your home. 

This works especially well for makeup artists specializing in bridal or minimalist everyday makeup looks.

#6. Data Entry 

Annual pay: $23,000 - $42,000

Data entry clerks are primarily responsible for entering data into databases, updating the databases, and retrieving the data upon request.

Since this is an entry-level work-from-home position, you can apply to it with just your high school diploma. However, you must be able to type quickly and accurately.

#7. Blogging

Annual pay: $21,000 - $66,000

On top of being fun and relatively easy, blogging can eventually become your main source of income. 

The key is to pick a topic that interests you and build your audience. For example, if you’re a stay-at-home mom looking for a work-from-home job, consider running a mom blog.

#8. Event Planning

Annual pay: $31,000 - $77,000

From brainstorming event themes to delivering post-event reports, event planners are on top of every part of planning events, including weddings, parties, conferences, and more.

As such, this is an ideal work-from-home job for multitaskers with exceptional organizational skills.

#9. Hairdressing

Hourly rate: $9 - $22

Although most hairdressers work in hair salons, hairdressing can also be a great work-from-home job.

Not to mention, being a home-based hairdresser helps you save money on rent and allows you to take as much time as you need to style your clients’ hair.

#10. Medical Coder

Annual pay: $31,000 - $57,000

Medical coders are responsible for researching, analyzing, and retrieving information from patient medical records.

Although this work-from-home job doesn’t require any work experience, you should have an up-to-date medical coder certification. 

#11. Coaching

Annual pay: $30,000 - $268,000

Depending on the coaching niche, coaches help clients to deal with personal or professional issues.

Since coaching isn’t a regulated profession, anyone with a gift of gab and motivational skills can become an online coach. 

#12. Babysitting

Annual pay: $17,000 - $37,000

Babysitters often have a range of different responsibilities depending on the family’s needs, which may include meal preparation, housework, tutoring, and more.

As such, babysitting might be the right work-at-home job for you if you’re patient, energetic, and, most importantly, love children. Not to mention, babysitting is also one of the top options if you’re looking for jobs for teens. 

#13. Technical Support Specialist

Annual pay: $33,000 - $74,000

Technical support specialists help clients resolve technical issues.

Although this work-from-home job is technical, such specialists receive on-the-job training, which means you don’t need prior work experience.

Part-Time Work From Home Jobs 

If you’re a student, stay-at-home parent, or just someone looking for a side gig, check out these 17 work from home jobs that you could do part-time:

#1. Search Engine Evaluation

Average salary: $40,697

Put simply, search engine evaluators provide feedback for search engines, which helps to improve search results, their accuracy, and quality.

The job requirements are simple - if you can use Google and have a laptop or a desktop computer, you qualify for the search engine evaluator position.

#2. Online ESL Teacher

Annual pay: $30,000 - $70,000

If you’re a native or near-native English speaker looking for a rewarding work-from-home job, consider teaching English as a second language. 

Not to mention, if you eventually choose to work in person, having an ESL certification allows you to get a work visa in different countries around the world!

#3. Tutor

Hourly pay: $10 - $40

Whether you’re a skilled guitarist or a math expert, you can turn any skill or knowledge you have into a part-time work-from-home job.

Depending on your niche, you can either tutor students via phone or a video call.

#4. Transcription

Hourly pay: $11 - $29

As a transcriptionist, you’ll be listening to voice recordings and accurately transcribing them into written text. 

These work-at-home jobs are particularly in-demand in legal and medical fields, so having industry-related knowledge can help you land the job. 

#5. Consulting

Average salary: $78,345

Do you have extensive knowledge of legal, business, marketing, or other matters? If so, consulting might be the ideal work-at-home job for you.

#6. Tax Preparation

Hourly pay: $10 to $25

Tax preparers help companies and individuals correctly file their tax returns. 

For this reason, being a tax preparer isn’t exactly a part-time job as you’ll likely only be in business before the tax return submission deadline. 

Nonetheless, it makes for a great side hustle.

#7. Voice Acting

Hourly pay: $15 to $104

If you have a flair for the dramatic, voice acting might be a great work-at-home job for you!

You can either try your luck in online voice acting auditions or look for freelance voice acting gigs on websites such as Fiverr - just make sure you have a high-quality microphone.

#8. Proofreading and Editing

Hourly pay: $12 to $31

Proofreaders and editors work on improving various types of content, including emails, articles, documents, and more.

So, if you have a knack for grammar and can make even the most awkward sentences flow, you might be perfectly suited for the role!

#9. Video Editing

Annual pay: $34,000 - $78,000

Essentially, video editors are responsible for turning raw footage into a finished video product.

So, if you know that pans and clean plates appear not only in the kitchen but also in video editing, this might be the right work-from-home job for you!

#10. Animation

Annual pay: $39,000 - $90,000

If you’re gifted at visual storytelling, you may want to consider a part-time work-at-home job as an animator.

Besides working for larger studios, you can work with smaller businesses, such as your favorite YouTube channels—especially if you’re just starting out and want to build your portfolio.

#11. Affiliate Marketing

Average annual pay: $60,341

If you already have a blog, website, or a large social media following, you may want to give affiliate marketing a go to get some additional income.

Essentially, once you sign up for an affiliate marketing program, you’ll be promoting specific products and getting a commission whenever someone clicks your link and makes a purchase.

#12. Social Media Management

Annual pay: $29,000 - $78,000

In short, social media managers help companies develop their online presence by implementing marketing strategies, producing high-quality content, and interacting with the audience.

So, if you're a social media guru that’s capable of quickly and effectively building a social media follower base from zero to thousands, you might be well-suited for a social media manager position.

#13. Translation and Interpretation

Hourly pay: $12 to $38

Although these two work from home jobs seem similar, here’s the difference - translators convert written text from one language to another, while interpreters translate spoken language. 

One thing to keep in mind, though, is that both the pay and the job demand largely depend on the languages you speak.

#14. Graphic Design

Annual pay: $31,000 - $66,000

Depending on the industry, graphic designers create memorable logos, informative brochures, eye-catching billboards, and other marketing materials.

So, if you’re skilled at creating inspiring and attractive visuals both manually and digitally, graphic design might be a great work-from-home job for you.  

#15. Writing

Annual pay: $29,000 - $100,000

If you have a way with words, you’re in luck - you can easily turn writing into a work-from-home job. 

Your pay will largely depend on your writing skills and experience, so make sure to pick a writing niche you feel most comfortable with.

#16. Stock Photography

Average monthly pay per one image: $0.02

From photo studios to the outdoors, photographers typically work in various locations.

However, alongside traditional photoshoots, you can also sell stock images for some extra income, especially if you already have a large portfolio.

#17. Virtual Assistance

Hourly pay: $10 to $26

Essentially, virtual assistants perform administrative tasks, such as scheduling meetings, managing emails, and answering phone calls remotely.

Although most companies don’t require virtual assistants to have a degree, outstanding organizational skills, computer proficiency, and years of work experience are a must if you’re aspiring to become a top-earning virtual assistant.

Work From Home Jobs for Seniors 

If you’re a senior looking for a remote job, we’ve got you covered!

Here are 6 work from home jobs that are ideal for seniors:

#1. Clinical Research Coordination

Annual pay: $37,000 - $67,000

If you have a degree in nursing and at least a couple of years of healthcare experience under your belt, clinical research coordination can be an excellent work-from-home job for you.

As a clinical research coordinator, you’ll be responsible for managing clinical trials, gathering research data, and administering questionnaires.

#2. Online Marketplace Sales

Average annual income: $34,388

Selling items online can be a hit or miss depending on the item, pricing, shipping expenses, and many other variables.

That said, if you’re looking for an additional source of income, you might want to give online sales a shot. For example, you can sell any used or new items you don’t need on eBay.

Alternatively, if you make handmade goods or have any vintage items, consider selling them on Etsy. 

#3. Baking

Annual pay: $20,000 - $37,000

If you have a passion for baking, consider turning it into a work-from-home job!

Besides offering delivery for individual baked goods, you can offer a catering service to supply weddings, birthday parties, and other events.

Just make sure to research the food laws in your area.

#4. Mock Online Jury

Pay per case: $20 - $60

This one’s for all Judge Judy fans out there - believe it or not, you can get paid for reviewing legal cases online! 

If you’re looking for a full-time job, however, you may want to look further - unlike most other work-at-home jobs, this one pays per gig, which typically takes up to an hour.

#5. Renting

Annual pay: varies based on the object, location, etc.

If you have a spare room, a car, or storage space you don’t use, rent it out for some additional income.

Alternatively, you can also rent out any other items that are collecting dust, including fishing gear, musical instruments, and such.

#6. Repair Services

Hourly pay: $14 to $50

Do you have a knack for repairing shoes, clocks, computers, cars, or other objects?

Well, if you’re looking for work-from-home jobs, consider becoming an in-home repairman!

5 Tips to Successfully Work From Home 

Although working from home allows for more flexibility, it also comes with its set of challenges. 

For example, since there’s no one to keep you accountable (except yourself), you may find it hard to avoid distractions or effectively manage your time.

So, here are 5 tips that will help you successfully work from home:

  • Stick to a schedule. If you find yourself struggling to manage your time and working late at night, try to set and stick to a working schedule. Not to mention, working during set hours helps you maximize your performance and maintain a good work-life balance.
  • Set boundaries. One of the biggest benefits of working from home is that it allows you to spend more time with your family. However, if your family members distract you from work, you may want to express your needs and set clear boundaries.
  • Have a dedicated workplace. No matter how tempting working from bed may seem, it’s important that you have a dedicated office space to maintain a better work-life balance.
  • Take breaks. Even if you’re working from home, make sure to take regular coffee and lunch breaks. This way, you can avoid overworking, which improves your efficiency and overall well-being.
  • Go outside. It’s easy to get isolated and inactive when you’re working from home, so make sure to regularly leave your house and get some fresh air. If working from home becomes too monotonic, you can always try a coworking space or a cafe.

Key Takeaways 

And that’s a wrap - now you have a good selection of the best work-from-home jobs to choose from. 

What’s more, you also know 5 tips that can help you successfully work from home!

Before you go, here are the key points mentioned in this article:

  • Some of the highest-paying work-from-home jobs include cloud architecture, product management, software engineering, and UX design.
  • If you’re looking for a work-from-home job that requires little or no experience, some of your options include blogging, babysitting, and event planning.
  • You can turn your hobby or interest, such as writing or graphic design, into a part-time job.
  • Baking, renting, and working as a mock online juror are some of the best work-from-home jobs for seniors.
  • If you’re working from home, make sure to set clear boundaries with your family members, stick to a routine, set up a dedicated workplace, regularly go outside, and take breaks.
